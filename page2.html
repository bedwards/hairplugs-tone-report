The Board, Dialed In

Every pedal’s face, drawn with its real controls, knobs set to the baseline-versatile starting point. A role and a nominal/preferred note for each. ← back to Page One

Squier → Keeley → Colour Box #1 → BBE Green Screamer → Colour Box #2 → Exploder → Warped Vinyl → Enzo → Vox AC15C1X (+ reverb)

Read each face as a starting point, not gospel — the chain is built so the two Colour Boxes voice each clipping stage (eq→gain→op-amp), the dirt stays low-gain and warm, and the Vox preamp does the crunch at conversation volume. Knob positions are drawn at the recommended settings below; dial from there by ear.

GUITAR · first EQ stage

1Squier CV '60s Tele Thinline

Your source, and the first EQ in the chain. Pick the voice here before a single pedal touches it — three single-coil flavours off the semi-hollow body.

Nominal: Volume & Tone full, bridge pickup. Preferred: bridge (cutting) or the middle/both position (jangly quack) for versatility; Volume 10 — there’s no treble-bleed, so keep it up to hold the highs; Tone ~8, backed a hair to take the brittle edge off the semi-hollow before the gain stages. Roll Tone (not Volume) to darken.

COMPRESSOR · even the source

2Keeley Compressor Plus

Evens the raw pickup dynamics and adds sustain before the gain stages; the blend keeps your pick attack so it doesn’t strangle the Thinline. First in line, on the raw signal.

Nominal: knobs at noon, HB. Preferred: Sustain ~9:00 (light), Level at unity, Blend ~10–11:00 (parallel / NY — dry attack passes), Tone ~1:00 to add back the highs compression dulls, toggle Single-Coil (longer release). A leveller, not a squash — it tames pick spikes and the feedback-prone body at the source.

CLEAN NEVE FRONT-END → into the BBE

3JHS Colour Box V2 — #1

First Neve front-end, kept clean. Its job: EQ the signal and use its output (Master) to slam the BBE’s clip — the Colour Box itself must NOT break up. Always engaged.

Nominal: EQ flat, STEP 1, LO, Master unity. Preferred: LO mode (clean headroom), STEP 1–2 (low — clean gain, no self-breakup), Pre-Vol low (don’t drive stage 2 into clipping), Master UP — this is what pushes the BBE (a hot, clean level into its input). EQ: Middle flat-or-slightly-cut (don’t double the BBE’s mid-hump), Bass + Treble slight up (add back what the TS scoops), Hi-Pass ~100–150 Hz to tighten the semi-hollow. Colour Box stays clean; the BBE does the distorting.

CLIP STAGE · kept neutral

4BBE Green Screamer V2

First clipping stage. Here it’s deliberately neutral so the two Colour Boxes do the voicing — its mid-hump is structural and can’t be nulled, so don’t add to it.

Nominal: all noon, Deep 0. Preferred: Gain ~9:00 (low), Tone noon (post-clip — sets a clean hand-off into Colour #2), Volume to unity-plus, Deep = 0 (flat; stop adding low-end voicing). Let Colour #1 push it and Colour #2 re-balance it — the BBE just clips.

CLEAN NEVE FRONT-END → into the Exploder

5JHS Colour Box V2 — #2

Second Neve front-end, between the dirts — also kept clean. Re-balances whatever the BBE’s mid-hump did, then uses its output (Master) to drive the Exploder’s clip. Your last pre-amp EQ point.

Nominal: flat, STEP 1, LO. Preferred: LO mode, STEP 1–2 (clean), Pre-Vol low, Master UP to push the Exploder, Bass ~1:00 to refill what the BBE scooped, Middle ~noon, Treble flat, Hi-Pass ~10:00 to keep the Exploder tight. Same rule: the Box stays clean, the Exploder distorts. Trade you accepted: with both boxes pre-clip, the Exploder’s own EQ + the Vox are your only post-dirt shaping.

MAIN CLIP · low-gain, Asym

6Beautiful Noise Exploder

Second/main clipping stage — used low-gain, asymmetric, no noise. Its 3-band EQ is post-clip, so it (with the Vox) is the final tone-shaper.

Nominal: knobs noon, Off. Preferred: Gain ~9–10:00 (low), Master to taste, clip switch Asym (even-harmonic, amp-like warmth), Low noon, Mid ~1:00 (body), Hi noon-or-south (tame the bright stack), W/N = 0 (white-noise off — Tone & Env then do nothing).

MODULATION · after the dirt

7Chase Bliss Warped Vinyl HiFi

Analog wow-&-flutter warble, placed after the dirt and before the delay (correct mod-before-time order). Subtle = lush movement; cranked = broken tape.

Nominal: Mix noon, low Warp. Preferred: Mix ~noon, RPM slow (~9:00), Warp ~9–10:00 (low = lush, not seasick), Depth low, Lag ~8:00 (raise toward its ~50 ms max for doubling — not slapback), Tone ~noon, wave-shapes sine. Warble smears the Enzo’s repeats downstream — keep it gentle unless you want woozy.

DELAY · clean slapback

8Meris Enzo

The delay. Run in Dry mode so it’s a clean echo, not a synth voice — your guitar passes through analog with one slap on top.

Nominal: synth on, knobs noon. Preferred: Dry mode (both Poly+Mono LEDs OFF), Pitch center (unison), Filter type Ladder-Highpass + Filter knob 0 (transparent), Mix ~9–11:00 (dry-favoured), Sustain low, Filter Env centre detent (off), Modulation min. ALT: Delay Level (Mix-ALT) below noon = single tap; Delay Feedback (Mod-ALT) min = one repeat. Time: tap by ear (lands ~180–240 ms, a wide slap) or MIDI CC#15 = 12 for 120 ms — then save to a preset.

AMP · preamp crunch + reverb/trem

9Vox AC15C1X

The amp — and where your conversation-volume crunch is won: drive the preamp (channel Volume up) with the Master low. Reverb and tremolo live here, not on the board.

Nominal: everything at noon. Preferred (low-volume crunch): plug into the TOP BOOST input. Top Boost Volume ~2–3:00 (preamp grind), Master Volume ~9:00 (low, for the room), Bass ~1:00, Treble ~11:00, Tone Cut ~10:00 (turning it up removes MORE treble — your fizz tamer, post-preamp), Reverb ~9–10:00 (light spring), Tremolo Depth 0 (off; raise Depth + set Speed when you want it), Normal Volume 0 (unused). Power & Standby ON.
